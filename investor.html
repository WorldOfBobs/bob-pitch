01 — Problem
Mac power is locked behind paywalls and steep learning curves.
100 million Mac users. Most of them barely using what they paid for.
The tools developers need — clipboard managers, color pickers, file converters, window managers — are sold separately at $20–40 each, totaling $300+/year
The best launcher on the market, Raycast, charges $96/year and still requires technical knowledge to get meaningful value from it
Non-technical users — the majority of Mac owners — have no accessible tools for automation, productivity, or customization
Every solution requires cloud accounts, API keys, subscriptions, or a learning curve most people don't have the patience for
The market has optimized for power users willing to pay. Nobody is building for everyone else — and everyone else is 80% of the market.

07 — Defensibility
Why this doesn't get killed by Raycast adding one feature.
Open Source Community
Raycast can't out-open-source us. Every contributor is a distribution channel. GitHub stars compound. A large open source project is very hard to replicate — it takes years to build.
Network Effect (Phase 2)
Bob Net creates social lock-in. Your contacts, your handle, your history — in the launcher. Switching means leaving your network. Raycast has no social layer to copy.
Price Moat
Raycast can't go free — they have 60 employees and a SaaS business model. We can stay free because the core is community-supported. Our pricing strategy is structurally impossible for them to match.
Speed of Shipping
12 apps built in weeks. Lean team moves faster than any VC-funded competitor. By the time Raycast adds local AI, we'll have shipped the next three phases.
